--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

extruder screw L/D (Length-to-Diameter) ratios, barrel cooling, and die head pressure.

- Zone 1 Heating: Controlled continuously to prevent premature resin melting.
- Melt Temperature: Maintained at 240 deg C to ensure stable viscosity.
- Cooling Lines: Must maintain a flow rate of 15 liters/minute at 2.5 bar pressure.

SECTION 1: PREAMBLE & REGULATORY AUDIT MANDATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This document presents the Q2 financial audit results for Apex PolyPlast India Private Limited. It details our segment performance, physical machine depreciation at the Silvassa Plant, and cost savings from running localized SLM co-pilots on on-premise servers, in compliance with the Indian Companies Act, 2013, and Indian Accounting Standards (Ind AS).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2: Q2 REVENUE AND SEGMENT PERFORMANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
During Q2, Apex PolyPlast generated a consolidated revenue of INR 208 Crore ($24.8M), representing an 8.4% Year-over-Year (YoY) increase. Segment performance includes:
- HDPE Conduit Sales: INR 98 Crore.
- PP Extrusion Fitting Sales: INR 65 Crore.
- Specialized Technical Polymers (Whitefield R&D): INR 45 Crore.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3: EXPORT LEDGERS AND REGIONAL SALES CHANNELS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Growth was supported by exports to South-East Asia and the Middle East, managed through our logistics and distribution centers in Noida, Chennai, and Ahmedabad.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4: INDUSTRIAL ASSET DEPRECIATION (SILVASSA & SANAND)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Under Schedule II of the Companies Act, 2013, our high-speed injection molding machine nozzles and heavy extrusion screws at the Silvassa Plant depreciated by INR 1.0 Crore due to continuous 24/7 high-temperature operational wear.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5: CLOUD VS. ON-PREMISE COMPUTE COST SAVINGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Our migration to localized, quantized SLM co-pilots running on our Whitefield R&D on-premise servers resulted in an 18% reduction in cloud computing and API service expenditures, moving workloads from costly public APIs to internal infrastructure.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6: LOCALIZED SLM OPERATIONAL ROI
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The transition to on-premise SLM co-pilots delivered high returns on investment (ROI):
- Reduced API billing fees by INR 22,00,000 in Q2.
- Mold flow design cycles were accelerated from 48 hours to 6 hours due to on-premise execution.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 7: CAPITAL INVESTMENT PLANNING (Q3 & Q4)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We recommend allocating INR 2.5 Crore in Q3 to expand on-premise computational server clusters at our Bengaluru R&D office, preparing for Phase 2 automation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 8: ACCOUNTS PAYABLE & RECEIVABLE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Outstanding receivables are managed on a strict Net-45 timeframe. Accounts receivable are tracked in our centralized CRM database to avoid collection lag.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 9: TAX PROVISIONS & DEDUCTIONS
