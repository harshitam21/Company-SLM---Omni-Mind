ADDITIONAL OPERATIONS ANNEXURE: DETAILED INDUSTRIAL PLASTIC EXTRUSION MACHINERY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-Clause 99.2.A: High-performance polymer extrusion requires strict control over 
extruder screw L/D (Length-to-Diameter) ratios, barrel cooling, and die head pressure.
- Single-Screw Extruders: Configured with a 30:1 L/D ratio for optimal melting.
- Zone 1 Heating: Controlled continuously to prevent premature resin melting.
- Melt Temperature: Maintained at 240 deg C to ensure stable viscosity.
- Cooling Lines: Must maintain a flow rate of 15 liters/minute at 2.5 bar pressure.
Failure to maintain these thermal metrics will cause polymer degradation, resulting 
in machine downtime and expensive purging compound requirements.

SECTION 1: PREAMBLE AND SCOPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This document establishes the comprehensive operational work models, safety guidelines, and code of conduct for all personnel of Apex PolyPlast India Private Limited. As a leading manufacturer of technical polymers, injection-molded electrical conduits, and high-density polyethylene (HDPE) fittings, our operations require continuous, high-precision industrial output alongside advanced computational R&D. This policy coordinates human resources protocols with strict safety and statutory requirements, ensuring compliance with the Indian Factories Act, 1948, and the respective state Shops and Establishments Acts.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2: DUAL-TEAM CLASSIFICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Due to the divergent nature of heavy plastics manufacturing, Apex PolyPlast maintains a strict operational division between our Technical/Engineering Teams and our Plant Labour/Production Teams:

2.1 TECHNICAL & ENGINEERING TEAM
Consists of Design Engineers, Material Scientists, Polymer Physicists, Systems Administrators, IoT Developers, and Executive Managers.
- Primary Locations: Corporate HQ (Mumbai BKC) and Polymer R&D Hub (Bengaluru Whitefield).
- Primary Focus: CAD mold design, mold-flow simulations, raw material testing, IoT thermal telemetry orchestration, and SLM B2B CRM integration.

2.2 LABOUR & PRODUCTION TEAM
Consists of Extruder Machine Operators, Blow-Molding Technicians, Resin Hoppers, Scrap Granulator Handlers, Quality Inspectors, and Shift Supervisors.
- Primary Locations: Extrusion & Injection Molding Plant 1 (Silvassa, D&NH) and Blow Molding Plant 2 (Sanand, Ahmedabad).
- Primary Focus: Heavy extrusion screw operations, raw resin loading (PP, HDPE, LDPE), cooling water line flow control, and physical product cataloging.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3: TECHNICAL & ENGINEERING TEAM WORKING MODELS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Apex PolyPlast supports modern software-driven operational workflows for white-collar whitefield and BKC technical engineering groups:

3.1 HYBRID WORK SCHEDULE
Eligible engineering and design staff may work under a 3-2 remote hybrid model:
- Three (3) Days Remote Work: Operating from a secured home network or designated remote office.
- Two (2) Days Physical Presence: Collaborating physically at the Bengaluru R&D Hub or Mumbai BKC Head Office.
- Hybrid schedules must be pre-approved by the Technical Director to ensure continuous on-premise CAD workstation coverage.

3.2 CORE COLLABORATION HOURS
All technical team members operating remotely must remain active, online, and readily accessible on corporate communication channels (Slack, Microsoft Teams, Email) between 10:00 AM and 4:30 PM Indian Standard Time (IST) on remote days.

3.3 APEX-SHIELD VPN AUTHENTICATION
Remote staff must access corporate design repositories and CRM databases exclusively through the secure Apex-Shield Zero-Trust VPN Gateway. Connecting to system directories or uploading CAD files via insecure, public Wi-Fi networks is strictly prohibited and monitored by the Cybersecurity division.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4: LABOUR & FACTORY FLOOR PRODUCTION TEAM WORKING MODELS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Continuous production is essential to prevent polymer solidification inside extruder barrels. Consequently, the Labour Team works on a 100% on-site, continuous physical rotating shift model:

4.1 ROTATING SHIFT MATRIX
Rosters are managed by Plant Operations Managers under the Factories Act, 1948. Shift schedules rotate weekly across three primary groups:
- Shift A (Morning): 06:00 IST to 14:00 IST (General Production & Quality Audits)
- Shift B (Afternoon): 14:00 IST to 22:00 IST (Heavy Extrusion & Packaging)
- Shift C (Night/Overtime): 22:00 IST to 06:00 IST (Extruder Purging & Night Extrusion)

4.2 OVERTIME POLICIES
Any hours worked in excess of the standard forty-eight (48) hours per week as defined by Section 51 of the Factories Act, 1948, will be compensated as overtime wages at double the ordinary rate of wages.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5: TECHNICAL LAB CODE OF CONDUCT (BENGALURU Whitefield)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Engineers modifying hardware components or software directories within the Bengaluru Whitefield R&D facilities must comply with technical lab protocols:
- Anti-Static Lab Gear: Personnel must wear anti-static lab coats and safety goggles when testing IoT sensors.
- ESD Wristbands: Electrostatic discharge (ESD) wristbands must be worn and grounded before touching exposed server nodes or fine-tuning local SLM computing boards.
- Prompt Calibration logs: Prompt modifications for CRM co-pilots must be logged in the system journal.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6: INDUSTRIAL PLANT SAFETY REGULATIONS (SILVASSA & SANAND)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Physical safety is paramount on the factory floor due to high temperatures and heavy machinery:

6.1 MANDATORY PPE
All factory labourers, supervisors, and visiting technical personnel must wear full Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):
- Steel-toed boots (protection against dropped heavy injection mold plates weighing up to 250 kg).
- High-temperature thermal gloves (engineered for handling extruder barrels heated up to 240°C).
- Industrial helmets and safety glasses.
- Heavy-duty ear plugs in granulator and cooling tower rooms where noise levels exceed 85 decibels.

6.2 EMERGENCY MACHINE SHUTDOWNS
In the event of an extruder backup, motor overload, or cooling water failure, any laborer is authorized to pull the Emergency Shutdown Lever located at the main power panel. Supervisors must be notified immediately.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 7: SHIFT HANDOVER PROTOCOLS (SHP)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Improper shift handovers lead to plastic solidifying in extruder screws, causing massive operational delays:
- Screw Purging: Outgoing operators must purge the extruder screw with purging compound if machine shutdown is required.
- Barrel Temperature: Operating temperatures must be verified in zones 1, 2, and 3.
- Parameter Log: Pressure indicators, cooling water flow rates, and screw RPM must be documented in the physical shift register and co-signed by incoming and outgoing shift operators.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 8: BIO-AUTHENTICATION AND TIMECARD LOGS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Apex PolyPlast uses integrated biometric tracking for all operations:
- Labour biometric logs: Shift workers must physically register fingerprints at gates at least 15 minutes before shift start.
- Tech biometric logs: BKC and Whitefield engineers log hours digitally via facial recognition panels.
- CRM Timecard Sync: All biometrics sync to the central HR CRM system to track attendance and compute EPFO contributions.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 9: VIOLATIONS AND DISCIPLINARY MEASURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Failure to comply with safety and remote work protocols will result in a structured disciplinary cascade:
- 1st Violation: Written warning from the Plant Safety Officer or HR Director.
- 2nd Violation: Mandatory 2-day suspension and retraining.
- 3rd Violation: Immediate termination of contract.

SECTION 10: APPENDIX A - EXTRUDER HEATING ZONE PROFILES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Below is the standard temperature profile matrix for Polypropylene (PP) and High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) extrusion screw operations:

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Material Type | Feed Zone (Z1) | Compression (Z2) | Metering (Z3) | Die Zone
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Polypropylene | 180 deg C      | 210 deg C        | 230 deg C     | 240 deg C
HDPE          | 170 deg C      | 195 deg C        | 215 deg C     | 225 deg C

SECTION 11: APPENDIX B - SAFETY INCIDENT REPORTING MATRICES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Any workplace injury, minor burn, or near-miss on the factory floor must be logged within 2 hours of occurrence:
- Immediate Action: First-aid administered at the plant clinic.
- Logging: Plant Supervisor inputs details in the CRM incident panel.
- Notification: Auto-routed to the Zero-Trust Security Agent and VP of Industrial Relations.
