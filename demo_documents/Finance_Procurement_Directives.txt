ADDITIONAL OPERATIONS ANNEXURE: DETAILED INDUSTRIAL PLASTIC EXTRUSION MACHINERY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-Clause 99.2.A: High-performance polymer extrusion requires strict control over 
extruder screw L/D (Length-to-Diameter) ratios, barrel cooling, and die head pressure.
- Single-Screw Extruders: Configured with a 30:1 L/D ratio for optimal melting.
- Zone 1 Heating: Controlled continuously to prevent premature resin melting.
- Melt Temperature: Maintained at 240 deg C to ensure stable viscosity.
- Cooling Lines: Must maintain a flow rate of 15 liters/minute at 2.5 bar pressure.
Failure to maintain these thermal metrics will cause polymer degradation, resulting 
in machine downtime and expensive purging compound requirements.

SECTION 1: PREAMBLE & FINANCIAL MANDATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This manual outlines the strict financial protocols and auditing controls for Apex PolyPlast India Private Limited. We aim to secure raw material supply lines while maintaining fiscal control. This document details our spend authorization thresholds, local SLM automation protocols, and compliance with the Goods and Services Tax (GST) Act, 2017.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2: CAPITAL EXPENDITURE (CAPEX) SPENDING LIMITS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
High-value purchases must go through a multi-tier authorization pipeline:
- INR 50,00,000 (Fifty Lakhs Rupees) and above: Requisitions for polymer raw materials or heavy machinery (extruders, injection molding units) require joint secondary authorization from the Vice President (VP) of Finance and the Managing Director (MD) at our Mumbai BKC headquarters.
- INR 10,00,000 to INR 50,00,000: Requires authorization from the Plant Director.
- All high-value contracts must be uploaded to the CRM ledger within forty-eight (48) hours of signing.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3: AUTOMATED LOCAL SLM EXPENSE APPROVALS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To improve operational speed on the factory floor, expenses under INR 50,000 (Fifty Thousand Rupees)—such as emergency spare parts, heating coils, or safety tools—can be approved automatically by local department SLM co-pilots, reducing processing lag.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4: RAW MATERIAL (POLYMER RESIN) PROCUREMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Polymers represent our largest operational cost. Raw resin orders must follow strict specifications:
- Polymers of Propylene (PP): Verified for melt-flow index.
- Polymers of Ethylene (HDPE): Verified for density parameters.
All bulk polymer purchases require a Certificate of Analysis (CoA) matching Apex standards.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5: GOODS AND SERVICES TAX (GST) COMPLIANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Under the GST Act, 2017, all purchase orders and invoices must include valid Goods and Services Tax Identification Numbers (GSTIN). No invoice will be processed for payment unless the supplier's GSTIN is verified and matching GSTR-2B returns are logged.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6: HSN CODE CLASSIFICATIONS FOR PLASTICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Correct HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) classifications must be presented on all invoices:
- HSN Code 3901: Polymers of Ethylene (HDPE / LDPE resins)
- HSN Code 3902: Polymers of Propylene (PP resins)
- HSN Code 8477: Machinery for working plastics/rubber (injection molding units, extruders)
Incorrect HSN codes will cause the CRM system to reject the transaction automatically.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 7: SUPPLIER VERIFICATION AND GSTR-2B MATCHING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The corporate Accounts division in Mumbai cross-references every supplier's filing on the GST portal:
- Active Status: Verification of GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B filings.
- Payment Block: Payment is blocked if the supplier fails to declare the transaction, preventing tax credit discrepancies.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 8: INVOICE AUDITING & CENTRALIZED CRM LEDGERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Every invoice is audited by our secure CRM system, verifying:
- Aadhaar/PAN compliance.
- Verified delivery logs at Silvassa or Sanand plants.
- Correct HSN matching and GST calculations.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 9: VIOLATIONS AND AUDIT FREEZES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Non-compliant invoices are placed in an Audit Freeze. Vendors with frozen records will have their payments delayed until the required documentation is uploaded and verified.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
