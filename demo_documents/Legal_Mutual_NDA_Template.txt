  polymer compositions, or automated extrusion software modifications developed 
  using corporate assets remain the exclusive intellectual property of Apex PolyPlast.

familiarize themselves with these statutory annexures. The central CRM database 
monitors compliance registers immutably. Any deviation, omission, or safety failure 
will trigger automatic notifications to the Zero-Trust Security Agent.

ADDITIONAL OPERATIONS ANNEXURE: DETAILED INDUSTRIAL PLASTIC EXTRUSION MACHINERY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sub-Clause 99.2.A: High-performance polymer extrusion requires strict control over 
extruder screw L/D (Length-to-Diameter) ratios, barrel cooling, and die head pressure.
- Single-Screw Extruders: Configured with a 30:1 L/D ratio for optimal melting.
- Zone 1 Heating: Controlled continuously to prevent premature resin melting.
- Melt Temperature: Maintained at 240 deg C to ensure stable viscosity.
- Cooling Lines: Must maintain a flow rate of 15 liters/minute at 2.5 bar pressure.
Failure to maintain these thermal metrics will cause polymer degradation, resulting 
in machine downtime and expensive purging compound requirements.

SECTION 1: PREAMBLE AND CONTRACTING PARTIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) is entered into under the Indian Contract Act, 1872, between Apex PolyPlast India Private Limited (Mumbai BKC HQ) and its external technical consultants, software developers, and B2B distribution partners, to secure proprietary data assets and protect trade secrets.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2: DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Confidential Information includes:
- CAD mold flow design parameters.
- Specific high-temperature extruder barrel pressure calibrations.
- Real-time heat telemetry logs from Silvassa extruder heating coils.
- Localized SLM co-pilot model weights and prompt templates.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND TECHNICAL FORMULATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Proprietary formulations—specifically flame-retardant polypropylene blending ratios—are protected. Any unapproved backup or fine-tuning extraction of Apex PolyPlast's local SLM weights constitutes a material breach of contract.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4: TECHNICAL TELEMETRY PROTECTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Extruder telemetry logs from the Silvassa and Sanand plants represent valuable proprietary operational profiles. External partners must not extract or access these telemetry streams without written authorization from the IT Security Director.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5: OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Receiving parties must maintain strict confidentiality, restricting data access only to employees with the required clearance credentials.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6: LIQUIDATED DAMAGES & BREACH PENALTY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
In the event of a documented breach, leakage, or unauthorized use of secure technical documents or model weights, default liquidated damages are valued at INR 4,20,00,000 (Four Crore Twenty Lakhs Rupees). This is without prejudice to Apex PolyPlast's legal right to seek immediate injunctive relief and punitive damages in the High Court of Bombay.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 7: ARBITRATION UNDER THE INDIAN ARBITRATION ACT, 1996
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Any disputes arising from this NDA shall be referred to sole arbitration in Mumbai under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, with the language of proceedings being English.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 8: SEVERABILITY AND TERM OF AGREEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If any provision of this agreement is found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force. The obligations of confidentiality shall remain active for five (5) years following the termination of the business relationship.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 9: EXECUTION AND GOVERNING LAW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This agreement is governed by the laws of India. Both parties acknowledge their understanding of the terms by executing this agreement.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Resolution: Executive Board reviews findings for legal escalation.
